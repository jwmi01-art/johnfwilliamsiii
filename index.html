Platform Scale-Up 2012 – 2018
EMU Attendance & Data Platform
QR-based attendance tracking system scaled from 2 to 140 personal mobile devices across a 23,000-student campus — eliminating dedicated hardware costs and enabling scanning anywhere on campus

Spearheaded the design and deployment of a QR code-based attendance and data collection system at Eastern Michigan University's Holman Success Center. The platform ran on personal smartphones — staff, students, and volunteers used a purpose-built mobile scanning app on devices they already owned, eliminating the need for dedicated hardware entirely. What began as a two-device pilot grew into a campus-wide system logging over 144,000 scans across dozens of event types — and produced documented academic outcomes.

  • Scaled from 2 to 140 active scanning locations using personal mobile phones running a purpose-built app — no dedicated hardware required, enabling flexible deployment anywhere on campus across study tables, Gen Ed events, textbook checkout, tutoring, and supplemental instruction programs
  • Demonstrated measurable GPA improvement for at-risk student-athletes and resulted in two Greek organizations being removed from academic probation
  • Adopted by the EMU Athletic Department to support NCAA Division I academic reporting requirements — providing reliable attendance and engagement data for student-athlete eligibility and compliance tracking
  • Built a QR-coded student ID database, designed validation workflows, and administered the full system end-to-end
  • Recognized by CodeREADr in a published case study — described as "an entrepreneurial graduate assistant" who made the program "enormously successful"
  • Saved the university $250K through an optimized shared reporting system adopted institution-wide as a template
